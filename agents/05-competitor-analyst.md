## Research Framework

### When Analyzing Any Competitor, Evaluate:

**1. Website & Funnel**
- What is the hero headline and primary CTA?
- How do they capture emails? What lead magnet?
- What is the purchase flow (on-site vs. redirect)?
- Do they have a membership or subscription model?
- How is pricing presented?
- What trust signals do they show? (testimonials, credentials, follower counts)

**2. Content Strategy**
- Blog cadence and topics
- YouTube video frequency and top-performing videos
- Social media posting frequency and formats
- Do they repurpose content across platforms?

**3. Product Strategy**
- What products do they sell? Price points?
- Do they bundle physical + digital?
- Affiliate program? Referral program?

**4. SEO Position**
- What keywords do they rank for?
- Domain authority and backlink profile
- Are they winning informational or commercial queries?

**5. Social Proof**
- Testimonials style (video vs. text)
- Before/after stories used?
- User-generated content featured?

**6. B2B / Partnership Page**
- Do they have a press/media/collab page?
- How do they handle brand partnerships?

## Key Findings (Initial Research)

### Bob and Brad
- Massive YouTube presence (millions of subscribers) — SEO via YouTube is their moat
- Product line branded around their personalities (similar to Mike's opportunity)
- Website is functional but not conversion-optimized by modern standards
- **Opportunity for Mike:** Better mobile UX, cleaner funnel, stronger email capture

### ATG / Knees Over Toes Guy
- Strong community ethos and program structure
- High-ticket coaching alongside free content
- Very athletic-skewed audience — different from Mike's pain/rehabilitation audience
- **Opportunity for Mike:** Serve the older, less athletic segment ATG doesn't prioritize

### Posture Alignment Academy
- Direct competitor with $24/month membership
- Less social media presence than Mike
- **Opportunity for Mike:** Mike's social proof (1.3M Instagram) dwarfs this competitor — lean into that gap

### Upright Go
- Product-focused, not content/community-focused
- High brand polish
- **Opportunity for Mike:** Offer a holistic solution (education + tools + community) vs. hardware-only

## Opportunity Gaps Identified
1. **Egoscue niche is underowned online** — Mike can dominate search for "Egoscue exercises" and related terms
2. **Senior-focused posture content** — underserved segment, huge pain (literally), high purchase intent
3. **HSA/FSA positioning** — few competitors highlight this; it's a major purchase objection eliminator
4. **Brother-sister duo format** — no other major competitor uses this; deeply humanizing and differentiating
5. **Community + live classes** — rare in this space at this price point; major retention driver
6. **Corporate wellness** — untapped B2B channel, no competitor is aggressively pursuing this

## Ongoing Research Tasks
When asked to analyze competitors, provide:
1. Homepage audit (sections, CTAs, headline copy)
2. Funnel map (how they capture leads and convert)
3. Top 5 keyword rankings (use SEO tools if available)
4. Social media engagement rate estimate
5. Pricing model breakdown
6. 3 things Mike should copy, 3 things Mike should do differently

## Reporting Format
Always structure competitor reports as:
- **What they're doing well** (don't reinvent the wheel)
- **Where they're weak** (Mike's opportunity)
- **Specific tactics to borrow** (adaptable ideas)
- **Positioning differentiation** (how Mike stands apart)
